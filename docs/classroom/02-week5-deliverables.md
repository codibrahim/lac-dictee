# Project Deliverables: Scoping & Architecture Defense

**Due: Apr 27 (Friday EOD)**

---

## Objective

Define your "Solo Venture" and defend your technical choices. A complete roadmap for your solo build.

---

## Design Sprint Steps

### Monday — Understand and Map
- Identify 1–3 real-world problems (AI/LLM solvable)
- Research: gather data, user feedback, examples
- Check: Do other apps solve this? How does your approach differ?
- Prepare: short summary, draft problem statements, Lean Canvas

### Tuesday — Sketch and Decide
- Choose the one problem for your Solo Project
- Use: empathy mapping, "How Might We", "Crazy 8s"
- Collaborate with peers for feedback
- Draft your Solo Definition of Done (DoD)

### Thursday — Prototype
- Plan the project, refine problem statement, select tech stack
- Create technical blueprint
- Justify why your approach is viable
- Do a quick prototype
- Start working on implementation

### Friday — Validate
- Present chosen problem, solution, and tech stack
- Get feedback from other founders
- Adapt & refine

---

## Required Deliverables

### 1. Problem Statement
```
Problem Title: [Concise Name]
Context/Background: [Current situation and context]
The Problem: [Specific issue, gap, or pain point]
Evidence/Data: [Data showing this is a problem]
Impact: [Who is affected? What are the consequences?]
Objectives/Goal: [What does success look like?]
```

### 2. Lean Canvas
A one-page business model for your automation.

### 3. User Empathy Map
Visual breakdown of your user's feelings, thoughts, and actions.

### 4. Manual Mockup/Sketch
Non-AI handmade visualization (paper, whiteboard, Excalidraw, or Miro).

### 5. Technical Blueprint
Flow diagram: **Input → Process (AI/Logic) → Output**

### 6. Solo Definition of Done (DoD)
Your personal checklist for what "Finished" looks like (includes baseline + your additions).

### 7. Pitch Deck
3–5 slides for Architecture Defense:
- Problem
- Solution
- Tech Stack
